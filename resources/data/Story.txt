

You wake to the sound of distant thunder.

The air is heavy with the smell of dust, old wood, and rain leaking through cracked window frames.
You are lying on the cold floor of a storm-battered mansion—vast, silent, and shifting faintly with each gust of wind.
You do not remember how you arrived here.

Somewhere deep within the house, a clock ticks.
Somewhere else, the wind seems to whisper.
You feel watched.

A chill settles on your skin, and you sense a strange truth:

The mansion remembers everyone who wanders inside.
And if you linger too long, it will remember you forever.

Your goal is simple:

Escape the mansion before 50 turns pass.

If the lights fade and the ticking stops, the house will close around you.

Act I — The Awakening (Entrance Hall)

You stand in the Entrance Hall, lit only by flashes of lightning through stained-glass windows.
A heavy Red Door stands to the east, its brass hinges rusted over.
Someone has scratched a message across the surface:

“Some doors are forgotten, some choose not to open.”

A side table holds an Old Map, edges curled with damp.
Faded markings circle a point in the Library, next to the words:
“Behind the shelf.”

A staircase rises north into darkness.

Despite the Red Door appearing locked,
a draft from the east tells you there is already a gap—
a narrow, usable passage that leads straight to the Library.

The house wants you to move.

Act II — The Upstairs Landing

The stairs creak under your weight as you reach the Upstairs Landing.
A huge portrait hangs crookedly, its frame broken.
Beneath it lies a Brass Key,
and beside that, a short Note:

“Check the bookshelf. It shifts when the time is right.”

The thunder outside intensifies.
You feel the house adjusting its weight—
like an old animal waking.

Act III — The Library

Warm yellow light pools across worn carpets as you step into the Library.

Shelves stretch to the ceiling—some full, some empty, some slanted dangerously.
A tired Caretaker sits beneath a green reading lamp.
His eyes lift weakly when you enter.

“Welcome… stranger.
Have you… seen my pocket watch?”

A small Pocket Watch rests on a desk nearby, its chain broken.
Beside it, a massive Bookshelf stands flush against the northern wall.
Scratches in the floorboards suggest it has been moved—recently.

Even without solving the shelf’s mechanism,
a narrow hidden passage stands slightly ajar to the north.
You could enter the Secret Chamber immediately…
but something urges you to explore the room first.

When you return the Pocket Watch, the Caretaker smiles faintly:

“Thank you… I forget things, but the house remembers.
Here—take this. It opens… something important.”

He presses a Library Key into your hand—
a useful but optional tool.

Act IV — Optional Mysteries (Side Areas)

Though unnecessary for escape, several parts of the mansion offer deeper insight into the house’s past.

Dining Room — The Ghostly Boy

A faint shape flickers at the long dining table.
A boy’s voice whispers:

“Thirsty… so thirsty...”

If you bring him Cup of Water from the Kitchen,
he fades gently, leaving behind an Old Room Key—
a relic tied to forgotten servants.

Servants’ Quarters — The Lost Maid

A pale figure sits on an old bed,
fingering a Silver Spoon as if searching for something.

When given the spoon, she nods:

“The passage… behind the shelf… it was meant to be secret.”

She vanishes in a gust of cold air.

Storage Room — Unused Lantern

A dusty cupboard hides a Broken Lantern.
When held, its flickering light seems almost afraid of the house.

Study Room — Old Papers

Documents mention vanished workers,
secret renovations,
and a phrase repeated throughout:

“The house remembers.”

Act V — The Secret Passage

Whether you open it with the optional Library Key,
or simply slip through the always-available gap,
you enter a narrow, cold corridor.

The walls tremble, dust falls from the ceiling.

Something deep inside the house is waking.

Act VI — The Secret Chamber (Final Area)

A final chamber lies beyond the passage.
A short stone pedestal holds a small chest of old coins.
A cold wind blows from a vent leading outside.

As you step toward the draft, the ceiling groans.
The ticking of the hidden clock grows louder, then stops entirely.

You climb through the vent into the stormy night.

Behind you, the house closes its doors.

Ending A — Escape

(If you reach before 50 turns)

You breathe cold, clean air.
Rain hits your face.
You survived.
But as you look back at the mansion,
for a moment you think you see a figure staring from the Library window.

The house remembers you.

Ending B — The House Keeps You

(If 50 turns expire)

The lights die.
Footsteps echo in the halls behind you.
Your vision dims as the air grows heavy.

One final whisper escapes the darkness:

“Stay…”

You are never seen again.
